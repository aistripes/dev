## Purpose

Build the go-to independent resource site for web application developers building AI-powered content systems, tools, and workflows.

The site exists to help developers who are working at the intersection of web development and AI — whether they're integrating LLMs into their stack, building content generation pipelines, shipping AI-native SaaS products, or figuring out how to make AI output actually useful at scale.

This is not a product site. It's an audience-first content platform that earns trust by being genuinely useful.

## Strategic Intent

### Primary goal

Drive organic traffic and build an engaged developer audience around AI-powered web development. Content volume and user growth are the priority. Monetization is deferred.

### Why this exists

The current landscape for developers building with AI is fragmented. Documentation lives in vendor silos (OpenAI docs, Vercel AI SDK guides, framework-specific tutorials). Practical, cross-cutting knowledge — the kind that helps a developer choose between approaches, avoid pitfalls, and ship faster — is scattered across blog posts, Twitter threads, and Discord channels.

This site consolidates that knowledge into a structured, searchable, community-improvable resource.

### The open source play

The entire content layer — taxonomy definitions, content schemas, and generated content files — lives in a public GitHub repository. The site builds from this repo.

This serves three strategic purposes:

1. **Credibility.** Developers trust what they can inspect. Open content signals transparency and invites scrutiny, which builds authority.
2. **Distribution.** Every GitHub star, fork, issue, and PR is organic reach. Contributors share what they contribute to. The repo itself becomes a discovery channel.
3. **Compounding quality.** Community contributions improve the taxonomy, catch errors, and fill gaps the core team misses. The content gets better as the audience grows.

## Overall Theme

### Editorial positioning

**"Build with AI, not around it."**

The site takes the position that AI works best inside well-designed systems with clear constraints — not as a freeform content generator. This philosophy runs through everything:

- Structured generation over freeform prompting
- Schema-driven pipelines over manual workflows
- Privacy-conscious, European-friendly infrastructure over default US cloud
- Open, inspectable systems over black boxes

### Audience

Web application developers who:

- Are building or integrating AI features into production applications
- Work with modern frameworks (Next.js, Nuxt, SvelteKit, Remix, Astro)
- Care about content quality, not just content volume
- Are evaluating tools, patterns, and infrastructure for AI-powered systems
- Lean toward privacy-conscious, self-hostable, European-friendly tooling

### Voice

Technical but accessible. Opinionated but fair. The tone of a senior developer sharing what actually worked — not a marketing team describing what could work.

- Show code, not just concepts
- Acknowledge trade-offs instead of selling silver bullets
- Prefer "here's what we tested" over "here's what you should do"
- Respect the reader's time — no filler, no unnecessary preamble

## Success Metrics (Phase 1)

Since monetization is deferred, success is measured by:

| Metric | Target (6 months) |
|---|---|
| Indexed pages | 5,000+ |
| Weekly organic clicks | 2,000+ |
| GitHub stars | 500+ |
| Community PRs merged | 50+ |
| Newsletter subscribers | 1,000+ |

These are directional, not commitments. The real signal is whether developers find the content useful enough to return, share, and contribute.

## What This Is Not

- **Not a blog.** There is no chronological feed. Content is structured, categorized, and evergreen.
- **Not a product site.** No pricing pages, no lead capture forms disguised as content. The site exists to serve developers, not to funnel them.
- **Not an SEO farm.** Every page must pass the test: "Would this be useful if search engines didn't exist?"
- **Not a solo project.** The open source model means the site improves with its audience, not despite them.
